History

Born in Lister Hospital in Stevenage, Hertfordshire (the same as Lewis Hamilton) I grew up in the garden-city called Letchworth in North Hertfordshire, England. I can recall spending time with my family and friends, sometimes playing my older brother's acoustic guitar when I was allowed, well, more like playing a toy than actually playing an instrument. However, by the age of 10 I was getting into quite a lot of trouble at school which was causing me problems at home as I would tend to have a lot of meltdowns. I wasn't a bad child according to my mum and dad, but I was having a lot of problems expressing my emotions in a constructive way. I was more DESTRUCTIVE, if you know what I mean.

Just before I started secondary school I was diagnosed with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) and High-Function Autism. To help cope with these, I started drum lessons at the Letchworth Drum Studio around the corner from my house. My mum and dad thought that 'drumming-out' my frustrations might be a good way to relieve some anger and stress. This worked for about a year until I got to the point of not enjoying it as much as when I first started so I gave it up for a while and thatâ€™s when I fell back into trouble. As it helped me out the first-time, I went back to drumming. It was like riding a bike.

Another year went past and I didn't feel the need to play drums anymore so instead I started to learn guitar (properly this time). I enjoyed the occasional jamming sessions with my Dad and Grandad Peter or my Uncle Ron who all play guitar.

When I first started playing guitar I was 13 and I would practice every day by watching either youtube videos or playing along with my favourite guitarist, Ed Sheeran. I even had my own youtube channel and was in a band at school. Unfortunately, I could only play four chords at the time and the other band members wanted me to play songs I didn't know the chords for which caused arguments. Eventually I left the band and carried on my practice at home.

My Guitars

As a present for my hard work, my Grandad Peter bought me my first electric guitar along with a strap, tuner and little practice 'Chord' brand amplifier (which I still use today).

I had seen in a local music shop window the exact guitar that I wanted in a light blue (as blue is my favourite color) and this guitar looked like a Fender Stratocaster which is the guitar shape my Grandad Peter plays. However, when I looked closer it was actually a 'Chord' brand guitar in the shape of a Stratocaster. Even better really because the price in the window was fairly cheap.

When my Grandad said he would buy it for me I was really excited. MY FIRST GUITAR! One that was mine, not borrowed from my brother or the school! So, on the first Saturday we were all able to get together, myself, Dad and Grandad Peter went into the shop and bought the guitar combo deal. I've played that guitar for hours-upon-hours since then.

About 2 years after, my sister-in-law had found both her Casio keyboard (with stand) and half-size acoustic guitar which she offered to give to me if I wanted them. I said yes and now had two guitars to learn on: my electric and this acoustic. I learned to play the keyboard for a short-while but didn't enjoy it as much as guitar so it was put away in the loft.

After a few years I wanted to buy an electro-acoustic guitar like the one Ed Sheeran plays, which is a Martin & Co. LX Ed Sheeran Signature. However they were a bit too expensive so I saved up and settled on the LX 1E standard model instead and eventually sold the other acoustic guitar. The Martin looks fantastic and plays beautifully.

Since my last year of school I have been writing my own songs and all of these I use my Martin to play on when I record the songs.

So I mainly use two guitars for all my practice and recordings:

1. Martin & Co. LX 1E Small Body Electro-acoustic guitar with pre-amp pickup.

2. Chord Stratocaster Electric Guitar with 3 single-pole pickups

My Equipment

I was bought an Apple MacBook Air Laptop for my 16th birthday which has software on it called 'Garage Band'. I started to learn how to use this software in my last year of secondary school and used that knowledge to play, sing, record and produce my own songs. I can't really sing but auto-tune is a god-send! .

At first, I was recording my voice and guitar on separate tracks through a microphone on my gaming-headphones but although this worked it wasn't ideal. After a visit to my Grandad Peter's house, we explored his music room and Dad showed me how to setup Grandad's 8-channel Behringer Xenyx mixer. This was great fun because he also showed me how to use EQ tricks to make my guitar sound like a bass and record it into my songs. I enjoyed that so much, I decided to buy myself a 2-channel Behringer Xenxy mixer and borrowed one of Grandad's Shure SM58 mics and mic stand.

Since getting a desk in my bedroom I have used an old tv to link up via HDMI to my laptop and plugged everything else in around the desk. It feels like my own little recording studio!

So, in summary, here's the list of my equipment:

1. 'Shure' SM58 Microphone with on/off switch

2. Standard XLR Balanced lead

3. Standard 4-point adjustment tri-pod Microphone stand

4. Behringer Xenyx 2-channel mixer with USB output

5. Apple MacBook Air with Garage Band App

6. 'Chord' 10 watt practice amplifier
